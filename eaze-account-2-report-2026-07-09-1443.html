Executive summary

Across the window (4 full settled days, Sun Jul 5 – Wed Jul 8, plus roughly 14.7 hours of Thu Jul 9 on the advertiser's PST8PDT clock at pull time), Eaze Account 2 spent $3,369.01 to generate 69 appointments and 14 leads. The account structure changed for the first time since tracking began: two new campaigns launched, a "RELAUNCH" of the Med Spa + Dental ABO campaign and a copy of the Aashish Maru UGC Reels campaign, both still in early ramp. Among appointment campaigns, Aashish Maru (original) remains the most efficient at $38.61 per appointment, ahead of the original Med Spa + Dental ABO campaign at $44.70; the two new campaigns are markedly more expensive so far ($79.53/appt and undefined, respectively) but have very little data yet. RPF 01 stays the account's only lead-generating campaign, converting at $18.97 per lead on a 4.46% CTR. Instagram again absorbed the majority of spend (57.3%, $1,929.72) at the account's weakest CTR (1.89%) and highest CPC ($8.46) among the major platforms. Most notably, the opportunity score jumped from 79 to 87 — reversing two consecutive declines — coinciding with the disappearance of the previously-flagged 7-point A+ Creative bundle recommendation from the list, consistent with it having been applied.

  • Best appointment engine: Aashish Maru [Do Not Touch] | Medspa UGC Reels (original) — $38.61 per appointment, 27 appointments, 1.95% CTR.
  • Best lead engine: RPF 01 - Dentists - WV - Images — $18.97 per lead, 14 leads, 4.46% CTR (the account's only lead-generating campaign this window).
  • Biggest drain: Instagram as a platform — $1,929.72 (57.3% of spend) at $8.46 CPC and 1.89% CTR, the account's least efficient major channel.
  • Notable change: Two new campaigns launched (5 total, up from 3) and the opportunity score jumped 79 → 87 as the previously-flagged A+ Creative bundle recommendation disappeared from the list — see Sections 02 and 11.

Action list

  1. Confirm the A+ Creative bundle actually landed. Its disappearance from the opportunity score list lines up almost exactly with the score's +8 jump (79 → 87) — worth a quick check in Ads Manager to confirm it was applied rather than simply expired or reset by the new campaign launches.
  2. Escalate the "Eaze Consulting" pixel's Lead match issue now — don't re-check again. Three consecutive pulls (today morning-equivalent readings and yesterday) all show 6.1 with zero PII keys. This has moved past "worth watching" into "needs a fix."
  3. Give the two new campaigns another day or two before judging them. "RELAUNCH" is running at $79.53/appt on just 4 results and "MOF - Aashish Maru Copy" has 0 results on $44.21 — both are too early to compare against the established campaigns' $38–45/appt range.
  4. Scale RPF 01 - Dentists - WV - Images. $18.97 per lead on a 4.46% CTR is still the account's most efficient engine by a wide margin; Meta's opportunity score continues to flag scaling it (+74% conversions estimated, 2 points).
  5. Prep a refresh for "UGC Reel Ad 05." CTR has now declined for five straight readings (2.51% → 1.83% → 1.83% → 1.53% → 1.36%) on the account's single biggest spend line ($838.49) — the longest sustained decline tracked yet.
  6. Address the Instagram CPC gap. Instagram takes 57.3% of spend ($1,929.72) at $8.46 CPC vs. Facebook's $3.69 — more than double. Test shifting budget weight toward Facebook placements or letting Advantage+ placements rebalance automatically.
  7. Exclude or reduce bids around 3 AM. 03:00-03:59 is the account's clear worst hour on both CTR (1.66%) and CPC ($9.41) this run — a clean candidate for a scheduling exclusion.
  8. Review Arizona targeting. Worst on both CTR (2.45%) and CPC ($8.11) in the top 10 states for a second straight report — worth narrowing the audience there or excluding until efficiency improves.

Overall direction: the account just made its first structural move since tracking began — two new campaigns in early ramp — and the opportunity score's sharp rebound suggests good execution on Meta's own recommendations. The near-term priorities are letting the new campaigns season before judging them, confirming the score jump reflects a real applied fix, and finally resolving the now three-times-confirmed pixel match-quality issue.
